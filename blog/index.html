How Consuming Cockblocks Your Creativity

May 2, 2025

I’m realizing that as a creative, and most importantly a CREATOR, one of the worst things you can do is consume. It clouds your mind, jumbles up your ideas, and if you consume just a little too much, you won’t know where your ideas start and theirs end. It also cockblocks your creativity.

Let me explain what I mean… I’ve heard it from multiple sources over the years that we should allow ourselves to get bored. In today’s landscape, it is very difficult to allow yourself to be bored because it’s so easy to reach for the main source of all sorts of distractions - our phone. You will never be bored when you have access to the whole world from this little device, and the hits of dopamine make it even more appealing to reach for. But creativity, in all forms, is born from boredom; from letting our mind roam because it has nothing else to do.

How does that connect to my opening statement? Well, when you consume, your mind is occupied. It’s being ‘entertained’ and filled up with other people’s ideas, which can sometimes serve as inspiration for your ideas to bounce off of. But the problem is, in today’s distraction era, we don’t even allow enough time after consuming to let our minds dissect what we’ve consumed to add to it. Which kills our opportunity for creativity.

Creativity requires brainpower; it demands space to grow, and so you must create that space. You must allow your mind to empty out and make room. You do this by letting yourself be bored. Next time you consume something interesting or that inspired some sort of reaction and thoughts from you, I want you to go and write down your thoughts on what you just consumed. It can be on why you agree or disagree, how you relate, or maybe it sparked a new idea in you. Instead of letting it go and consuming something new, sit on that one thing, elaborate, and allow your mind to create something with it. It may be nothing after all, but I think it’s important for you to at least explore it.
